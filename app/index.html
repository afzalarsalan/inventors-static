About Us

Clements Inventors’ Club is truly unlike any other club at Clements High School because, unlike the majority of other clubs, it is neither an honor society nor does it define itself for competition. It is not about pushing students to accomplish someone else’s goals but is rather to empower students to accomplish their own goals and allow students to discover a way to be truly successful in life after high school and even after college.

About Us

This club is designed to help students to:
  1. Get their start in inventing
  2. Develop students' skills and knowledge
  3. Protect their ideas
  4. Delve into the entrepreneurship aspect of the invention business - the tips to make money from inventions.

The club aims to allow for students to share with each other their ideas, to work together on constructing those ideas, and to share past experiences and knowledge in designing products.

Houston has the world's largest inventors' association outside of Japan: the Houston Inventors Association (HIA). The Association is comprised of thousands of entrepreneurs and hobbyist inventors. It has members and advisers with great expertise and knowledge. Our Inventors' Club will tap into the resources of these professionals allowing high school students to transform their everyday small ideas that make daily life easier into concrete inventions. Additionally, these professionals can deliver presentations to members on the business side of entrepreneurship including identifying product opportunities, marketing, applying research and development, patenting/copyrighting, etc.

The Clements Inventors' Club (CIC) will provide Clements students with the information, education and tools to get started now itself on pursuing what many students perceive as their future. It plans to have a meeting every week where members can have access to some sort of lab or workshop space and get to work on their inventions. The meetings will be calm and relaxed and encourage students to pursue their interests. If the lab environment is not adequate at Clements, we will have a bus or carpooling plan that would drive CIC members to a more suitable environment with better technical tools, computers, and mentors to help teach design concepts, how to use modeling software, etc. CIC will bring in professionals from various fields and companies who can present their advice and also serve as mentors to club members. These people will also explore the business side of Entrepreneurship. Professors/Students from the Bauer College at U of H or at Rice can provide guidance and education. Additionally, students will be able to seek guidance through local Patent Lawyers as well as more experienced inventors and students.

In addition to these weekly workshops, we will also stay in sync with the HIA and update members on their calendar of events.
